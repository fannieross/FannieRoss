FAQ

Frequently Asked
Questions

Everything you need to know about working with me. If you have additional questions, just email me and I will be happy to answer.

How do I know if my book is ready for marketing?

Your book is ready if it is professionally edited with no typos, grammar issues, or plot holes. It should have a professional cover design appropriate for your genre. It needs to be available for purchase or preorder. You should have a clear understanding of your target audience. And you need to be willing to participate in your own marketing efforts. If you are unsure, email me your book details and I will give you honest feedback about readiness.

What if my book is in a very niche or unusual genre?

Perfect. Niche books often market better than broad appeal books because the target audience is more defined and passionate. I have successfully marketed everything from cozy knitting mysteries to academic philosophy texts to dystopian romance. Unusual does not mean unmarketable. In fact, the more specific your niche, the easier it often is to find and reach your ideal readers.

Do you guarantee specific sales numbers?

No, and you should be skeptical of anyone who does. Book sales depend on too many variables including book quality, genre saturation, pricing, timing, and market conditions. What I do guarantee is effort, strategy, transparency, and proven tactics. I guarantee that I will work diligently on your behalf and provide regular reporting on all activities and results. My track record speaks for itself with an average 340% sales increase across all campaigns.

How involved do I need to be?

Moderate involvement produces the best results. You will need to respond to my emails within 48 hours on weekdays, provide requested materials and information promptly, review and approve strategies before implementation, participate in some activities like social media posts and email newsletters when needed, and give feedback when asked. I handle the heavy lifting. You provide necessary input and materials.

What if I have no social media presence at all?

That is completely fine. Many of my most successful clients started with zero followers and minimal online presence. We build everything from scratch using proven growth strategies. Starting from zero sometimes works better because we avoid bad habits and build correctly from the beginning. You will have a professional, engaged following before you know it.

How long until I see results?

Most campaigns show initial results within 2 to 3 weeks. Social media growth starts immediately. Email list building begins week one. Review generation typically starts week 2 to 3. Sales increases are usually visible by week 3 to 4. Sustained momentum builds over 60 to 90 days. Quick wins happen early. Sustained success builds over time.

Do you work with traditionally published and self published authors?

Yes, both. About 60% of my clients are self published and 40% are traditionally published. The strategies differ slightly, but both types of authors benefit enormously from supplemental professional marketing. Even traditionally published authors often need additional marketing support beyond what their publishers provide.

What if I have multiple books or a series?

Even better. Series and multiple books create more marketing opportunities through cross promotion, bundling, and reader funnels. I offer specific series marketing strategies that maximize the value of having multiple titles. The read through rate optimization alone can dramatically increase your overall revenue.

How much do your services cost?

Investment levels vary based on the scope and duration of services you need. Every author's situation is unique, so I create customized proposals with transparent pricing based on your specific goals and budget. Email me with your book details and what you are looking to achieve. I will respond within 24 to 48 hours with a detailed proposal including timeline, deliverables, and investment required. I believe in complete transparency about costs before you commit to anything.

Why do you only communicate via email?

Email provides massive benefits for both of us. You get a complete written record of everything we discuss. You can review proposals on your schedule. All deliverables and timelines are clearly documented. There is accountability built into every interaction. Nothing gets forgotten or misremembered. All details are captured accurately. It is a professional paper trail that protects both of us and ensures nothing falls through the cracks.
